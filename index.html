Oscar Box Office

Can an Oscar nomination or win help a movie financially?

2019 Academy Awards

By Daisy Williams
Published March. 13, 2020 8:00 PM

Every year the Academy Awards rolls around we all ask, “Does it mean anything?”. For some movies make a mere 1% of their earnings post Oscars whilst some see a 1/3 increase. This year, there wasn’t a huge jump due to a best picture nomination. Below, the chart shows each nominated film trajectory from release day and from the start of 2020. All numbers are domestic box office
NOTE: Marriage Story and The Irishman are excluded because Netflix doesn't release Box Office numbers.



All past winners have all had an increase after their win. Some of the biggest jumps are films that weren’t favorites and surprise winners including Greenbook and The Artist. Something like, Moonlight is unexpected considering the drama surrounding it. The King’s Speech was a clear favorite winner earning over 50% of the votes quicker than any other film in history, so the narrative around it predicting that it was going to win, reads with it jumping prior to the awards.

Parasite
The first foreign film to win Best Picture was highly helped by its Best Picture winner. Adored by critics, also being the highest rated on Rotten Tomatoes by critics, Parasite was a clear critical darling. Earning a third of its gross box office after the Oscar, of the past 10 winners Bong Joon Ho’s movie is the second highest peak post Academy Awards. Parasite is clearly a winner from the Oscars in every sense.

Once Upon a Time In Hollywood
Quentin Tarantino's ninth movie, Once Upon a Time in Hollywood (OUATIH) made $142,502,728.00 domestic box office. With an 85% rotten tomatoes rating, and an audience score of 70, the movie was the 3rd highest rated of his films, cracking the top 20 of 2019. The third provisional film of his career, OUATIH was a summer hit but it’s Oscar nominations and win for supporting acting Brad Pitt, who had an exceptionally charming award season, didn’t help its box office. The film was the first to come out of the nominations and played to audiences like a summer blockbuster rather than an Oscar buzzy movie.



Ford vs. Ferrari
Everyone’s favorite dad flick made 1.1% of its income after the Oscar. After the Damon/Bale flick piqued when it premiered in October- a normal trajectory for a pre-holiday film, the award buzz, nominations and win didn’t affect the course for the film. The film was the highest audience reviewed of all of the discussed nominated film, receiving 98 fresh on rotten tomatoes. It won some technical awards but nothing mainstream audiences really cared for.




Little Women
Little Women was the poster-girl of female nominations this award season. Conversation around no female nominations for Best Director revolved around Greta Gerwig’s snub rather than sharing the light with other directors like Farewell’s Lulu Wang or Honeyboy’s Alma Har'el. This put a lot of attention on the film. Little Women saw a little bit of a boost post Oscar, but the remake did only get released on Christmas Day, playing for 46 days before the awards. You can say it is because of the win for best costume and nomination for best picture but seemingly appears its director’s exclusion from best director pushed it into ‘must see’ status. Oscars prestige definitely helped this classic.



Jojo Rabbit
The controversial yet hilarious Jojo Rabbit delighted critics with a 94 rotten tomatoes score. The film was nominated for Best Picture along with Production Design, Supporting Actress, Costume Design and Film Editing and took the prize of Adapted Screenplay. A conversation began around director/writer/actor, Taika Waititi, a highlight for indigenous people. The film that opened to five cinemas was the lowest earner at the box office of all seven films but saw an increase before the Academy Awards.



1917
The favorite for Best Picture did not see a big jump after the Oscars. However, the World War I movie saw an increase after it’s surprising win at the Golden Globes on January 5th. Sam Mendes one shot masterpiece left with some technical awards, tying first with three wins, but it’s hard to predict how drastic the film would have increased if it won the Best Picture Oscar. It made three times more than the actual winner, but will it make as much of a splash in VOD or more importantly in history?



Joker
Joker broke records in 2019, being the ninth top grossing film and also the ninth out of nine films to hit the billion-dollar barrier. The superhero flick made the most of all the movies on the list. However, an Oscar win for Best Actor and being the most nominated film of 2020 did not affect the box office. The controversial subject nature helped the box office more than any award buzz
